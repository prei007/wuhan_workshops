Many secondary school students use AI writing assistants to revise their essays. 
Yet AI feedback does not influence revision in a uniform way. 
Rather, the educational effects of AI depend on how students engage with the feedback.
Some students critically evaluate AI suggestions, compare them with their own intentions,
and use them to reorganise and strengthen their arguments. Others accept suggestions 
uncritically or restrict their revisions to surface-level corrections. The central theoretical
proposition is that AI feedback does not directly improve writing quality. Instead, 
it changes the processes through which students evaluate, regulate, and revise their work. 
Differences in these underlying processes generate differences in the quality of revision 
and, ultimately, in learning outcomes.
